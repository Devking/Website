Hello There!

This is my personal website. I do wish to warn you that this site is text heavy. I've decided to stay away from fancy graphics and just stick to a flat, minimal design that emphasizes writing.

By the way, I coded this site entirely on a text editor, and don't use code or frameworks from anyone else! Go ahead and resize the window horizontally (if you're on a full browser) and see how responsive the page is!

I am still working on further expanding or redesigning this website. A few of my more graphically-inclined friends highly suggest that I say less and show more. While this may be true, it really wouldn't reflect who I am that much. In this society when '140 characters' is the limit, I really feel like we're missing out on the joy of really reading. For that reason, I've purposely padded these pages with a bit more content than one would usually find on a programmer or designer's personal webpage. Still, I hope to figure out a clever and more aesthetically pleasing way to present and emphasize all this text. The quest continues...

Who Am I?

I am currently a student at NYU Polytechnic School of Engineering, pursuing a Dual-Degree in Computer Science (Master's) and Computer and Electrical Engineering (Bachelor's). I also minor in Philosophy of Science.

My primary specialization is in Neuro-Cognitive Engineering, or more specifically, on topics of Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Brain-Machine Interface. My undergraduate work was primarily in computer architecture, parallel processing, and analog electronic design, leading up to work with bioelectrics.

Furthermore, I am independently researching topics on Neurophilosophy and Organic Computing. By studying advances in neuroscience and engineering, I hope to reimagine arguments in philosophy of mind, including topics on knowledge representation, identity, consciousness, and mind-body dualism. On the side, I also enjoy working on topics of existentialism and philosophy of science. I strongly believe in the need for a deeper synthesis between the sciences and the humanities.
